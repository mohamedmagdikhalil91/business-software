Company Overview

Urban Canopy sells smart indoor planters equipped with IoT sensors that monitor soil moisture, light, and temperature. A companion app — powered by a machine learning model trained on plant biology data — guides users through the entire growing cycle and auto-orders monthly seed and nutrient kits. The result: fresh herbs and microgreens on your kitchen counter, with zero guesswork.

Founding Story

Origin

Divya Nair was a botanist turned AI researcher who spent three years at Bayer CropScience training models to predict plant health from sensor data. Felix Braun was a hardware engineer at Bosch who had spent too many weekends watching his partner's herb garden die. They met at a climate tech hackathon in 2022, built a prototype overnight, won the hardware prize, and launched Urban Canopy in early 2023. Their first batch of 200 Starter Kits sold out in four days.

Products & Services

  • Starter Kit (€149) — smart planter with built-in sensors, grow light, and companion app
  • Monthly Subscription (€29.99) — seed pack + nutrients + growing guides, auto-curated by the app
  • B2B Office Installation — sensor array + maintenance service for offices and co-working spaces
  • Grow Coaching Add-on (€9.99/month) — live video consultations with a botanist
  • Urban Canopy App — iOS and Android; free tier + premium subscription

Business Challenge

⚠️ Current Challenge

Urban Canopy has a growing app satisfaction problem: their NPS is 34 — below category average and dragging on word-of-mouth growth. Additionally, Divya and Felix have never built a formal financial model; investors are asking for a path to profitability. They need an NPS analysis of the raw survey data, a 3-year financial model with unit economics and break-even analysis, and a professional investor summary document.
