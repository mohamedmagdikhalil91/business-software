Company Overview

Zenith Wellness operates a premium yoga and pilates studio in Munich alongside an online wellness platform offering live and on-demand classes. Corporate wellness packages are a growing revenue stream, with clients including two tech companies and a healthcare provider.

Founding Story

Origin

Ananya Sharma had been teaching yoga in Munich for 15 years and had built a loyal following, but her studio was perpetually full with a waiting list and no digital presence. Marcus Weber, a product manager at a fitness tech startup, saw a clear digital opportunity. They combined Ananya's teaching reputation and community with Marcus's tech experience to launch Zenith Wellness in 2021 — a hybrid studio-platform now serving members across 14 countries.

Products & Services

  • In-Studio Classes — yoga, pilates, meditation; drop-in €18, monthly €120
  • Online Subscription Platform — live + on-demand classes, €29.99/month
  • Corporate Wellness Packages — bespoke programs for company teams, from €500/month
  • Retreats — seasonal 3-day wellness weekends at partner venues (sold out, 2 per year)
  • Teacher Training Programme — 200-hour yoga teacher certification (annual intake, 12 students)

Market & Competitive Landscape

The global wellness market is valued at €80 billion and growing at 12% annually. Post-pandemic demand for mental health and fitness solutions has accelerated. Online fitness platforms (Peloton, Alo Moves) set the digital benchmark.

Business Challenge

⚠️ Current Challenge

Zenith Wellness currently manages all studio bookings via a WhatsApp group. This has caused 11 double-bookings this quarter, three instructor scheduling conflicts, and at least one membership cancellation due to the confusion. Marcus wants a proper tracker and a financial projection for opening a second studio location in Schwabing.
