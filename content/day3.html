📐 Unit Economics — The Per-Unit View

Unit economics asks: what does it cost to deliver one unit of value, and what do we earn from it?

  • Unit = 1 cup of coffee / 1 video / 1 hoodie / 1 class
  • Unit Revenue = selling price per unit
  • Unit Cost (COGS) = what it costs to produce one unit
  • Unit Margin = Revenue − COGS per unit
  • Margin % = (Unit Margin ÷ Unit Revenue) × 100
Margin % = (20 − 8) ÷ 20 × 100 = 60%

📊 Core Excel Concepts — Before You Touch a Formula

Three ideas that separate a working model from a broken one:

1 · Formulas vs. Manual Entry

Never type a calculated number directly into a cell. Type the formula instead. When your inputs change, every result updates automatically — no re-typing, no errors from forgetting to update a cell.

# ❌ Manual — breaks every time you change the price
D4 = 1497     ← someone typed this by hand

# ✅ Formula — always correct, zero maintenance
D4 = =B4*C4  ← units × price, auto-updates

2 · Relative vs. Absolute References

When you drag a formula down, Excel adjusts the row numbers automatically (relative). Sometimes you want one cell to stay fixed — lock it with $ signs (absolute).

# Relative — row changes as you drag down
=B4*C4               ← B5*C5, B6*C6 …

# Absolute — cell stays fixed (e.g. a VAT rate in C1)
=B4*$C$1            ← always multiplies by C1, no matter where you drag

# Shortcut: press F4 after clicking a cell to cycle through $-modes

3 · Cell Naming for Readable Formulas

Give important cells a name (Formulas → Define Name). Your formulas become self-documenting — no more guessing what $B$2 means.

# ❌ Hard to read — what is B2?
=D4 / $B$2

# ✅ Self-documenting — name B2 as "BoxPrice"
=D4 / BoxPrice

👁️ See Example: Unit Economics — ByteBite Margin Walkthrough Example

Let's trace through a real unit margin calculation for ByteBite, the meal-prep company in your portfolio. Watch how a single price change cascades through every number.

✅ Healthy Unit Economics

ByteBite: Premium Tier — €14.99 / box

Line Item Per Box (€) % of Revenue
Selling Price 14.99 100%
— Ingredients 4.20 28%
— Packaging 0.80 5%
— Delivery 2.50 17%
Variable Cost Total 7.50 50%
Contribution Margin 7.49 50%

At €14.99, ByteBite covers all variable costs and has €7.49 left per box to cover fixed costs (rent, salaries, software). This is healthy for a D2C food business.

❌ Margin Squeeze — Price War Risk

ByteBite: Budget Tier — €9.99 / box

Line Item Per Box (€) % of Revenue
Selling Price 9.99 100%
— Ingredients 4.20 42%
— Packaging 0.80 8%
— Delivery 2.50 25%
Variable Cost Total 7.50 75%
Contribution Margin 2.49 25%

Same cost base, €5 lower price. Now only €2.49 remains. If delivery costs rise by €0.50 (very common), margin drops to 20%. One bad quarter and fixed costs aren't covered.

THE EXCEL FORMULA CHAIN
B2 = 14.99           ← Selling price (input — change this cell)
B6 = B3+B4+B5      ← Variable cost total (auto-sums rows 3–5)
B7 = B2-B6         ← Contribution margin (€)
C7 = B7/B2         ← Contribution margin (%)

Change B2 from 14.99 → 9.99 and every derived cell updates instantly. This is why formulas beat manual calculation.

Takeaway: Build your financial model so that changing one input (price) flows through the entire sheet automatically. No copy-pasting. No recalculating. That's the power of cell references.

Hands-On: Build Your P&L Skeleton

~60 minutes · Use the provided company dataset

📁
Your File: [company_slug]_financial.xlsx Open your company's financial model template (e.g. bytebite_financial.xlsx). The structure is already built — your only job is to fill in the yellow cells with the correct formulas. Blue cells are pre-filled inputs; green cells will auto-calculate once your formulas are correct. Read the Instructions sheet first!

Fill In the Financial Model

  1. Open [company_slug]_financial.xlsx. Read the Instructions sheet — it explains the colour coding and what each section means.
  2. Switch to the Financial Model sheet. Read through it top to bottom before writing any formula.
  3. Section 1 — Revenue: For each yellow cell in the Amount column, write a formula multiplying the Quantity (blue) by the Unit Rate (blue). Example: =B6*C6
  4. In the Total Revenue row, write a SUM formula over all your revenue lines.
  5. Section 2 — COGS: Repeat the same pattern — Qty × Rate for each cost line, then a SUM for Total COGS.
  6. Section 3 — Gross Profit: Write =Total Revenue − Total COGS. Then write the Gross Margin % formula: =Gross Profit / Total Revenue. Format as %.
  7. Section 4 — Fixed Costs: SUM all fixed cost lines.
  8. Section 5 — EBIT: Write =Gross Profit − Total Fixed Costs.
  9. Section 6 — Break-Even: Write the formula shown in the hint cell: =Fixed Costs / (Price per Unit − Variable Cost per Unit).
  10. Add an IF formula below EBIT: =IF(EBIT > 0, "✅ Profitable", "⚠️ Loss").
✅
Quality Check Change one "Unit Price" in column C. Watch: Revenue, Gross Profit, Margin %, and Net Profit should all update automatically. If they do — your formulas are linked correctly. If they don't — check your cell references.
