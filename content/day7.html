Grading Rubric

Total: 100 points · Breakdown across 5 dimensions

📌
Assessment Philosophy You are not graded on business viability — your company doesn't need to be a billion-dollar idea. You are graded on your professional use of business software tools and your ability to communicate a business story clearly and persuasively.
Criterion Excellent (90–100%) Good (70–89%) Adequate (50–69%) Insufficient (<50%)
1. Slide Quality & Branding
20 pts 		Consistent Slide Master throughout, branded colours/fonts, visual clarity, ≤30 words per slide, professional design Mostly consistent, minor formatting inconsistencies, clean but not fully polished Some branding applied but inconsistent, text-heavy slides, limited visual design No evident Slide Master use, random formatting, text-heavy, unprofessional appearance

25 pts 		Complete P&L with working formulas, linked charts in deck, margin % calculated, clear unit economics, scenario analysis P&L complete with mostly working formulas, charts present, minor formula errors P&L structure present but some values hardcoded, limited formula use No financial model, or model with fundamental errors / hardcoded values

Peer Feedback Guide

After each pitch, the audience gives structured feedback. Be specific — be kind — be honest.

The Feedback Framework: WWW + EBI

✅ What Worked Well (WWW)

Identify 2–3 specific things that impressed you. Be precise — not "the deck looked nice" but "the revenue chart on slide 6 made the growth trajectory immediately clear."

📈 Even Better If (EBI)

Give 1–2 constructive suggestions framed as improvements, not criticisms. Not "the financials were confusing" but "Even better if the unit margin was shown per product to let investors compare SKUs."

🎨

Design Feedback

Was the branding consistent? Did the visuals support the story or distract from it?

💰

Financial Feedback

Did you understand the business model? Were the numbers credible?

📢

Delivery Feedback

Was the presenter confident? Did the narrative flow? Were time limits respected?

🏢

Story Feedback

Would you invest (or recommend a friend to use this product)? Why or why not?
