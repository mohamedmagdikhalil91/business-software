📊 Embedding Excel Charts in PowerPoint

Don't copy-paste images of charts — link them so they stay live:

  1. In Excel, select your chart → Copy (Ctrl+C)
  2. In PowerPoint, go to Home → Paste Special → Paste Link
  3. Now if you update the Excel data, the chart in PPT updates too
  4. Right-click chart in PPT → Update Link to refresh manually

✍️ Data Storytelling Rules

  • One insight per slide — if you have 3 points, make 3 slides
  • The headline IS the insight — not "Revenue Chart" but "Revenue grew 40% in Q1"
  • Visual > Text — use a chart where possible. Use a number in large font for single KPIs
  • Colour with purpose — highlight only what matters in your brand colour. Everything else is grey
  • 30-word rule — no slide should require more than 30 words to be understood

👁️ See Example: Slide Headlines — Insight Title vs. Topic Title Example

The single biggest upgrade you can make to any presentation: replace topic titles with insight titles. See the same slide transformed.

✅ Insight Title — Tells the Story

Revenue Slide — What the Audience Remembers

BYTEBITE · INVESTOR DECK · MAY 2025
Revenue tripled in 6 months — driven by repeat orders, not new customers
3× ↑
Jan   Feb   Mar   Apr   May   Jun

The title is the takeaway. Even someone who glances at this slide for 2 seconds understands: revenue grew 3×, and the growth driver matters (repeat orders = retention, not just acquisition).

❌ Topic Title — Tells Nothing

Revenue Slide — What Gets Forgotten

Slide 7
Revenue Chart
Jan   Feb   Mar   Apr   May   Jun

"Revenue Chart" just describes what the audience can already see. It forces them to do the analytical work themselves — and different people will draw different conclusions. The presenter has lost control of the message.

The Formula: Topic Title → Insight Title
Topic Title (❌) Insight Title (✅)
Revenue Chart Revenue tripled in 6 months — driven by repeat orders
Customer Data 72% of revenue comes from customers who ordered 3+ times
Market Overview The €4.2B meal-kit market is growing 18% YoY — and under-served in DACH
Team Slide Our founding team has 20 years of combined food logistics experience

Takeaway: Before every slide title, ask: "What do I want the audience to believe after seeing this slide?" That answer is your title. The chart/table/bullet points are just the evidence that supports it.

Hands-On: Build Your Investor Deck

~90 minutes · Start from your Day 1 Slide Master template

📁
Start from your template Open your [CompanyName]_Template.pptx from Day 1. Go to File → New from this template. Your brand is already set. Now focus entirely on content.

Build Slide-by-Slide

  1. Slide 1 — Cover: Company name (large), tagline (smaller), your name, and today's date. Use the Title layout from your Slide Master.
  2. Slide 2 — The Problem: State the problem in one sentence at the top. Add a visual: icon, photo, or SmartArt showing the pain point. No bullet lists.
  3. Slide 3 — Our Solution: One sentence. One image or icon. Then: 3 key features in a horizontal SmartArt or icon row.
  4. Slide 4 — Market Opportunity: Use a large number (e.g., €4.2B market). Add a brief source line. One sentence of context.
  5. Slide 5 — How We Make Money: Simple diagram: [Customer] → [Pays for X] → [We earn Y]. Can use SmartArt or shapes.
  6. Slide 6 — Traction / Key Metrics: Import your KPI chart from Excel using Paste Special → Link. Add a 1-sentence headline insight above the chart.
  7. Slide 7 — Financials: Import your P&L summary from Excel. Show Revenue, Gross Profit, and Net Profit for Month 1. Keep it simple — one table or chart.
  8. Slide 8 — The Team: Your name + role + 1 relevant strength. If solo: explain why you're the right person for this problem.
  9. Slide 9 — The Ask: What do you need? (Not just money — also: clients, partnerships, expertise.) How will you use it?
  10. Slide 10 — Thank You / Contact: Company name, your name, email, and a memorable closing line.
✅
Quality Check — The 30-Second Flip Test Give your deck to a classmate. They flip through all slides in 30 seconds. Can they tell you: what the company does, who the customer is, and how it makes money? If yes — your deck passes the visual clarity test.

📌 Day 6 Deliverable — Final Pitch Deck

10-Slide Investor Pitch Deck

This is your final submission artifact. It must stand on its own — someone should be able to read it without you in the room and understand your business.

  • Exactly 10 slides following the structure from today's session
  • Built using your Day 1 Slide Master template — consistent branding throughout
  • At least 2 charts linked from your Excel financial model (Day 3/4)
  • Slide headlines are insights, not labels (e.g., "Revenue grew 3x in 90 days" not "Revenue")
  • Maximum 30 words per slide (titles excluded)
  • Speaker Notes filled for every slide — at least 2 sentences per slide for the instructor's reference
