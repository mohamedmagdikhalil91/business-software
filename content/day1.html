Theory: Branding & the Slide Master

The 20% that explains 80% of professional PowerPoint

🎨 What is a Brand (in practice)?

A brand is not just a logo. It's a system of visual rules that makes every document you produce instantly recognisable and trustworthy.

  • Colour palette — 2–3 colours, used consistently
  • Typography — 1–2 fonts, with clear hierarchy (heading vs. body)
  • Logo placement — same corner, same size, every slide
  • Tone & voice — the feeling your text creates

🖥️ What is the Slide Master?

The Slide Master is a hidden "control room" inside PowerPoint. Any change you make there applies to every slide in your deck — automatically.

  • Set fonts once → all slides update
  • Set background → all slides update
  • Add logo → appears on every slide
  • Define layouts → ensure consistent structure

Hands-On: Build Your Slide Master

~45 minutes · Work in pairs, deliver solo

🤖
Gemini AI Co-pilot Moment Open Gemini and ask: "I'm building a brand for [your company name]. Suggest a professional colour palette (3 colours as hex codes) and 2 Google Fonts that match the brand personality." Use its suggestions as your starting point — but you decide the final design.

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Open a blank PowerPoint presentation. Save it as [CompanyName]_Template.pptx
  2. Go to View → Slide Master. Click the first (top) thumbnail — this is your Master Slide.
  3. Open Slide Master → Fonts. Create a custom font set: choose one font for Headings, one for Body text.
  4. Open Slide Master → Colors. Create a custom theme: set your brand's primary, secondary, and accent colours.
  5. Insert your company name as a text box in the footer area of the Master Slide. This will appear on every slide.
  6. Add a colour bar or shape to the Master Slide that reinforces your brand (e.g., an orange bottom strip).
  7. Click on the Title layout thumbnail. Customise the title slide — larger logo area, full-bleed background option.
  8. Click on the Content layout thumbnail. Ensure the body placeholder font matches your brand font.
  9. Close Master View. Create 3 test slides using different layouts. Do they all look consistent?
✅
Quality Check Create a new slide. Without touching any formatting, it should already have your brand fonts, colours, and footer. If it does — your Slide Master is working correctly.

Assignment

Due: End of Day 1 · Submit as .pptx file

📌 Day 1 Deliverable

Brand Book — 5-Slide Presentation

Using the Slide Master you just built, create a 5-slide Brand Book for your company. This is a professional internal document that defines your brand's visual identity — not just what it looks like, but why it was designed that way.

  • Slide 1 — Title Slide: Company name, tagline, and founding year. Uses your Title layout.
  • Slide 2 — Our Colour Palette: Show your 3 brand colours with their hex codes and names. Add 1–2 sentences explaining why you chose these colours — what feeling or value do they communicate for your company?
  • Slide 3 — Typography Guide: Show your heading font and body font with example text and sizes. Explain in 1 sentence why this font combination fits your brand (e.g. "We chose Inter for its clean readability — it reflects our no-nonsense, data-driven approach").
  • Slide 4 — Brand Voice: 3 adjectives that describe your brand, with a short example sentence in your brand's tone.
  • Slide 5 — Visual Rules: Define at least 2 rules your brand always follows and at least 2 rules it never breaks (e.g. "We never use more than 2 fonts on one slide", "We never use bright neon colours"). These rules should be grounded in the rationale you wrote on Slides 2–3.
⭐ Bonus: Use SmartArt for the Colour Palette or Typography slide. Keep it under 30 words per slide — force yourself to be visual.
