Theory: Branding & the Slide Master

The 20% that explains 80% of professional PowerPoint

🎨 What is a Brand (in practice)?

A brand is not just a logo. It's a system of visual rules that makes every document you produce instantly recognisable and trustworthy.

  • Colour palette — 2–3 colours, used consistently
  • Typography — 1–2 fonts, with clear hierarchy (heading vs. body)
  • Logo placement — same corner, same size, every slide
  • Tone & voice — the feeling your text creates

🖥️ What is the Slide Master?

The Slide Master is a hidden "control room" inside PowerPoint. Any change you make there applies to every slide in your deck — automatically.

  • Set fonts once → all slides update
  • Set background → all slides update
  • Add logo → appears on every slide
  • Define layouts → ensure consistent structure

🎨 Colour Representation

Every colour on screen is defined by numbers. Three systems — all describing the same colour:

HEX  #F39200
RGB  R 243 · G 146 · B 0
← three ways to write the same orange
  • Hex — 6-digit code (#RRGGBB). First 2 = Red, middle 2 = Green, last 2 = Blue. The format designers and developers use — paste it directly into PowerPoint's colour picker.
  • RGB — same colour as three 0–255 numbers. Both formats are interchangeable; use whichever the tool accepts.
  • Theme colour grid — PowerPoint's 10-slot palette (2 Text/BG + 6 Accents + 2 Hyperlinks). Always set your brand colours here — every chart, shape, and SmartArt then uses your palette automatically.
# Set a theme colour in PowerPoint
Slide Master → Colors → Customize Colors
→ Click Accent 1 → More Colors → Custom tab → paste hex code

🔤 Typography

Typography is the single biggest visual signal of professionalism. Four rules:

  • Serif vs. Sans-serif
    Serif (Georgia, Times) → traditional, authoritative  ·  Sans-serif (Calibri, Montserrat) → modern, clean.
    For slides: sans-serif almost always wins.
  • Font pairing — use exactly 2 fonts: one for headings, one for body. Never more.
  • Weight & style — each tool has a job:
    Bold signals importance  ·  Italic signals emphasis  ·  All Caps signals formality.
    Use each sparingly — if everything is bold, nothing is.
  • Size hierarchy
    Title 40pt  ›  Section 26pt  ›  Body 18pt  ›  Caption 12pt
💡
Free brand fonts: fonts.google.com — search by personality (e.g. "modern", "elegant"). Download the .ttf → install on Windows → appears in PowerPoint immediately.

📦 Placeholder Types

Placeholders are the pre-defined content zones inside every Layout slide. They are what makes a Slide Master actually useful — content snaps into position automatically.

📝

Title

The main heading zone. Usually top or centre of the slide. Controlled by Slide Master → Title placeholder.

📄

Content

Multi-purpose zone that accepts text, tables, charts, images, or SmartArt. Click the icon inside to choose type.

🖼️

Picture

Image-only zone with fixed position and size. Keeps your layout intact even when different images are used.

🔢

Footer / Date / Slide №

Auto-populating zones in the bottom bar. Set once in Insert → Header & Footer — applies to all slides.

# Add a placeholder to a Layout slide
Slide Master view → select a Layout slide → Insert Placeholder dropdown
→ choose type (Content / Picture / Text / etc.) → draw the zone on the slide

Charts & SmartArt in PowerPoint

Turn numbers and structures into visuals — without leaving PowerPoint

📊 Inserting Charts

PowerPoint has a built-in chart editor. You don't need Excel — just go to Insert → Chart, pick a type, and edit the sample data directly in the pop-up spreadsheet.

  • Column / Bar — compare values across categories (e.g. revenue by product line)
  • Line — show a trend over time (e.g. subscriber growth month by month)
  • Pie / Doughnut — show part-of-whole (e.g. revenue split by tier). Use sparingly — only when you have 2–4 slices and the percentages matter
  • Combo — two chart types on one axis (e.g. bars for revenue + line for margin %)
# Insert a chart
Insert → Chart → choose type → edit data in pop-up grid

# Apply brand colours to chart
Click chart → Chart Design → Change Colors → pick your theme palette

🧩 SmartArt

SmartArt converts a plain bullet list into a professional diagram — process flows, hierarchies, cycles, and more — in a few clicks.

  • List — visual bullet points with icons or colour blocks
  • Process — left-to-right steps (e.g. onboarding flow, supply chain)
  • Cycle — circular flow showing repeating stages (e.g. feedback loop)
  • Hierarchy — org chart or decision tree
  • Relationship — Venn diagrams, opposing ideas
# Insert SmartArt
Insert → SmartArt → pick category + layout → type text

# Convert existing bullet list to SmartArt
Right-click text box → Convert to SmartArt
💡
When to use which Use a chart when you have numbers to show. Use SmartArt when you have a structure or process to explain. Use a plain bullet list only when neither applies — most bullet lists are actually better as SmartArt.

Hands-On: Build Your Slide Master

~45 minutes · Work in pairs, deliver solo

🤖
Gemini AI Co-pilot Moment Open Gemini and ask: "I'm building a brand for [your company name]. Suggest a professional colour palette (3 colours as hex codes) and 2 Google Fonts that match the brand personality." Use its suggestions as your starting point — but you decide the final design.

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Open a blank PowerPoint presentation. Save it as [CompanyName]_Template.pptx
  2. Go to View → Slide Master. Click the first (top) thumbnail — this is your Master Slide.
  3. Open Slide Master → Fonts. Create a custom font set: choose one font for Headings, one for Body text.
  4. Open Slide Master → Colors. Create a custom theme: set your brand's primary, secondary, and accent colours.
  5. Insert your company name as a text box in the footer area of the Master Slide. This will appear on every slide.
  6. Add a colour bar or shape to the Master Slide that reinforces your brand (e.g., an orange bottom strip).
  7. Click on the Title layout thumbnail. Customise the title slide — larger logo area, full-bleed background option.
  8. Click on the Content layout thumbnail. Ensure the body placeholder font matches your brand font.
  9. Close Master View. Create 3 test slides using different layouts. Do they all look consistent?
✅
Quality Check Create a new slide. Without touching any formatting, it should already have your brand fonts, colours, and footer. If it does — your Slide Master is working correctly.

Assignment

Due: End of Day 1 · Submit as .pptx file

📌 Day 1 Deliverable

Brand Book — 5-Slide Presentation

Using the Slide Master you just built, create a 5-slide Brand Book for your company. This is a professional internal document that defines your brand's visual identity — not just what it looks like, but why it was designed that way.

  • Slide 1 — Title Slide: Company name, tagline, and founding year. Uses your Title layout.
  • Slide 2 — Our Colour Palette: Show your 3 brand colours with their hex codes and names. Add 1–2 sentences explaining why you chose these colours — what feeling or value do they communicate for your company?
  • Slide 3 — Typography Guide: Show your heading font and body font with example text and sizes. Explain in 1 sentence why this font combination fits your brand (e.g. "We chose Inter for its clean readability — it reflects our no-nonsense, data-driven approach").
  • Slide 4 — Brand Voice: 3 adjectives that describe your brand, with a short example sentence in your brand's tone.
  • Slide 5 — Visual Rules: Define at least 2 rules your brand always follows and at least 2 rules it never breaks (e.g. "We never use more than 2 fonts on one slide", "We never use bright neon colours"). These rules should be grounded in the rationale you wrote on Slides 2–3.
⭐ Bonus: Use SmartArt for the Colour Palette or Typography slide. Keep it under 30 words per slide — force yourself to be visual.
