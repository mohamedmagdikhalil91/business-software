Theory: Project Management & MS Planner

Lightweight tools for lightweight teams

🗂️ What is a Kanban Board?

Kanban (Japanese: "visual signal") is a method for managing work as it moves through stages. Each task is a card. Cards move across columns (buckets) from left to right.

🏗️ MS Planner — What it Does

  • Plans — one Plan per project or team
  • Buckets — the columns on your Kanban board
  • Tasks — individual cards with title, assignee, due date, priority
  • Labels — colour-coded categories for task types
  • Charts view — automatic progress visualisation
  • Schedule view — timeline view of all tasks

💡
The 3 Golden Rules of Kanban 1. Never have more than 3 tasks "In Progress" at the same time — focus matters. 2. A task isn't "Done" until it's reviewed by someone else. 3. When you add a new task, check if it's already there under a different name.

👁️ See Example: Kanban Task Quality — Well-Defined vs. Vague Task Cards Example

Your team uses a Kanban board to manage work. Here's the same task written two ways — see which one actually moves work forward.

✅ Well-Defined Task Card

ByteBite — Sprint 4 Card

🔥 High Priority
Write product description copy for 3 new meal plans (Vegan, Keto, Classic)

Write 80–100 words per plan. Tone: friendly & energetic. Include one differentiating benefit per plan. Reference brand voice doc in Google Drive.

📎 Brand Voice Doc ✅ Done = reviewed by lead
👤 Assigned: Priya  ·  📅 Due: Thu 15 May  ·  ⏱ Est: 2h
  • Specific deliverable: 3 descriptions, 80–100 words each
  • Clear "done" definition: reviewed by lead
  • Linked reference: brand voice doc attached
  • Realistic estimate: 2 hours
  • One owner — no ambiguity
❌ Vague Task Card

ByteBite — Same Task, Poorly Written

Normal
Content

Write stuff for the new meal plans

👤 Team  ·  📅 Soon
  • Title "Content" — what content? How many? Which plans?
  • "Stuff" — no length, no tone, no format specified
  • "Team" as owner — everyone's responsible = no one is
  • "Soon" — not a deadline. Will be missed.
  • No definition of done — how do you know when to move it?

Takeaway: A good task card answers: Who does what, to what standard, by when, and how do we know it's done? If you can't answer all four, the card isn't ready to be worked on.

Hands-On: Build Your Planner Board

~60 minutes · Work individually, then share with a partner

🤖
Gemini AI Co-pilot Moment Ask Gemini: "I'm the founder of [your company]. I have a final investor pitch in 2 days. List the 10 tasks I need to complete before the pitch, in priority order." Use the output to populate your Planner board.

Build Your Operations Board

  1. Open Microsoft Planner at tasks.office.com (or from Teams).
  2. Create a new plan called "[Your Company] — Operations".
  3. Create the following buckets (columns): Backlog | This Week | In Progress | Review | Done
  4. Add at least 3 tasks per bucket. Each task must have a title, due date, and be assigned to yourself.
  5. Create labels for at least 3 categories relevant to your company (e.g., Design, Finance, Client, Content).
  6. Set up Labels (the coloured dots on each task).
  7. For tasks in "In Progress", add a checklist inside the task card — break each task into 2–3 sub-steps.
  8. Open Charts view (top right). Take a screenshot — this is your board's status report.
  9. Share your plan with a classmate. Assign them one task as a "reviewer" — they must check it and move it to Done.
✅
Quality Check Switch to Schedule view. You should see a timeline with all tasks placed on a calendar. If tasks show as "Late" (red), update their due dates — but acknowledge why they were late in a task note.

📌 Day 5 Deliverable

Project Roadmap to Pitch Day

Build a Planner board that maps out everything your company needs to do between now and the Day 7 pitch. Think like an operations manager.

  • 5 buckets structured as a workflow (not just "Done" and "Not Done")
  • Minimum 15 tasks total, covering all remaining work for your final pitch
  • All tasks have due dates (none beyond Day 7), priority levels, and labels
  • At least 3 tasks have checklists with sub-steps
  • Charts view screenshot showing task distribution across buckets
